Mapping and Visualizing Live Data Feeds

The United States Geological Survey provides Google Earth Real-Time KML Feeds to display information about earthquakes. This webmap displays a summary of recently-identified earthquakes. Feeds are updated every minute for the past 30 days. Each marker provides information regarding the location, magnitude, tsunami status, and link for further information.



NASA provides active fire data to demonstrate in Moderate Resolution Imaging Spectroradiometer (MODIS) (MCD14DL) and Visible Infrared Imaging Radiometer Suite (VIIRS) 375 m (VNP14IMGTDL_NRT). This webmap uses KML data from MODIS 1 km and identifies fires within the past 24 hours at a global scale. Looking at the map, one can assume a great concentration of fires in certain parts of the world, like Brazil, South America and across parts of West, Central, and East Africa.
References: United States Geological Survey, National Aeronautics and Space Administration.
